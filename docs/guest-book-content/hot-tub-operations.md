# Hydrotherapy Jacuzzi

This isn't a standard hot tub — it's a **premium hydrotherapy massage jacuzzi**. Five dedicated seats, three independent jet zones, individual throttle controls per zone. Think deep-tissue hydro massage, not just warm water. Take your time with it.

## Seating Layout

The jacuzzi seats **5 people** across **4 distinct zones**:

1. **Love seat** (2 people) — shared jet zone
2. **Front single seat** — shared jet zone with love seat
3. **Therapy seat** (across from control panel) — independent jet zone, 2nd most powerful
4. **Control panel seat** — the deepest, most powerful hydrotherapy position

---

## Controls Quick Reference

| Button / Knob | What It Does |
|---------------|--------------|
| **Jets 1** | Main jets — has **High / Low** toggle |
| **Jets 2** | Secondary jets — simple **On / Off** |
| **Jets 3** | Therapy seat jets — **On / Off** (independent zone) |
| **"Blower" button** | Actually controls the **lights** (labels are swapped) |
| **"Lights" button** | Actually controls the **blower / bubbles** (labels are swapped) |

> **Heads up:** The **Blower** and **Lights** buttons are switched from what the labels say. Press "Blower" for lights. Press "Lights" for the bubble feature (oxygenator).

## Jet Zones

### Love Seat & Front Single Seat (Shared Jets)

These two seats **share one set of jets**. There is a **sideways throttle knob** located near the love seat that directs the jet flow:

| Throttle Position | Jets Go To |
|-------------------|------------|
| Turned toward love seat | Love seat gets full flow |
| Turned toward single seat | Single seat gets full flow |
| **Middle position** | Flow split between both seats |

We keep it in the **middle** so both seats get jets.

There are also **foot jets** between the love seat and front single seat — a nice bonus most people don't notice at first.

### Therapy Seat (Across from Control Panel)

This seat has its own **independent jet zone** controlled by **Jets 3** (On/Off). It also has its own **throttle controls** to dial intensity up or down. This is the **2nd most powerful** hydrotherapy position — serious targeted massage.

### Control Panel Seat (The Power Seat)

The deepest seat in the jacuzzi with the **most powerful jets**. This is the full hydrotherapy experience:

- Completely independent jet zone with dedicated nozzles
- Its own **throttle control** for intensity (low to full power)
- Multiple adjustment knobs for directing individual jets exactly where you need them

If you've got sore muscles, tension, or just want to feel what a real hydrotherapy massage is like — this is the seat.

## Temperature Guide

The jacuzzi goes up to **104°F** — but that's intense. Here's what we recommend:

| Temp | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **98°F** | Standby (how we keep it when not in use) |
| **100–101°F** | Longer sessions — still warm enough to feel great, cool enough to stay in comfortably. You'll still want to sit on the edge periodically to cool down |
| **103–104°F** | Maximum heat — you'll last about 5 minutes before needing to get out. Great for a quick intense soak |

### Heating Tips

- The tub heats about **2°F per hour with the lid closed**
- **Plan ahead** — if it's at 98° and you want 101°, close the lid and give it about 90 minutes
- If you overshoot and set it too high, it takes a while to cool back down — easier to warm up gradually
- Set your target temp, close the lid, and go do something else while it heats

## Before You Get In

**Please rinse off before getting in the jacuzzi.** Body oils, sunscreen, and lotions cause foaming and make maintenance much harder. Just throw on your swimsuit, take a quick rinse in the indoor shower, and walk out to the tub. (Sorry — no warm outdoor shower yet.)

## The Lid

The cover has a **metal assist bar** that helps guide it on and off. There's also a **bench stand at the back** of the tub — when you flip the lid up, it slides back and rests on the stand automatically.

### How to open/close

1. **Stand at the middle of the lid** — this gives the most even leverage
2. Flip the lid up so it's open halfway (hinged in the middle)
3. Guide it back — it should slide onto the bench stand on its own
4. **Lift evenly** to avoid bending the metal assist bar
5. To close, reverse the process — pull from the middle, guide it down evenly

> **Please be gentle with the metal guide bar** — it's been repaired once already. Even pressure from the center is the key.

The cover has some cosmetic rips and tears on the outer layer — that's just the protective shell. The inner foam core has been **resealed and re-wrapped**, so insulation is solid.

## Longer Stays: Keeping the Water Fresh

If you are staying multiple nights and plan to use the jacuzzi regularly, please add shock after each use. This keeps body oils, lotions, and normal use from making the water cloudy or stale.

1. When you are done soaking, leave the cover open.
2. Add **two capfuls of spa shock** from the supply cabinet near the hot tub.
3. Turn the jets on and let them run with the cover open for about **10 minutes**.
4. Then turn the jets off and replace the cover.

Please do not add extra shock beyond this. If the water ever looks cloudy or smells off, message the host and we will help.

## Tips

- There are tons of buttons, switches, toggles, and adjustment dials **all around the tub and by every seat** — explore and find your setup
- Throttle knobs control intensity and direction, not on/off — use the Jets buttons to activate, then fine-tune with the knobs
- Jets 1 is the primary system (high/low); Jets 2 is supplemental (on/off); Jets 3 is the therapy seat (on/off)
- **Always replace the cover when you're done** — keeps the heat in and the tub clean
- Feel free to play around — you won't break anything by turning knobs
