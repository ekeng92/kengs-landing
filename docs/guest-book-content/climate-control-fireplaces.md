# Climate Control — Fireplaces & Heaters

The house has multiple fireplace units across the living room and bedrooms: **electric** and **propane**.

---

## Electric Fireplaces (Bedrooms)

Plug it in — but it won't turn on by itself. Here's the part guests miss:

1. **Plug the unit in** — you may hear a beep confirming power
2. Look for a **small metal grate on the top-right** of the unit
3. Under that grate is the **power button** — press it to turn on
4. There's a **second button** for **High / Low** heat setting

> **Common issue:** Guests plug it in, hear the beep, and think it's broken. The power button is hidden under that little metal grate — easy to miss.

If the unit seems unresponsive after plugging in, look for a **reset button** on the back or underside and press it, then try the power button again.

---

## Propane Fireplace (Living Room)

Located in the main living area. The propane supply valve is at the **bottom of the unit**.

### Lighting the Propane Fireplace

1. Turn the knob to **Pilot** mode
2. **Hold the knob down** for about 5 seconds (this lets gas flow to the pilot)
3. While still holding the knob down, **click the starter button**
4. Once the pilot lights, continue holding for a few more seconds
5. Release the knob, then **turn it to a higher setting**

> **Tip:** If it doesn't catch on the first try, repeat the process. If you're having trouble, don't hesitate to call your host.

### Temperature Settings (1–5 Dial)

The numbered dial (1–5) is **temperature-based**, not a simple on/off:

| Setting | Behavior |
|---------|----------|
| **1** | Lowest — cycles on occasionally |
| **2–3** | Recommended — comfortable and efficient |
| **4** | Higher heat, more frequent cycling |
| **5** | Maximum — stays on continuously |

The fireplace **turns on and off automatically** based on the ambient temperature in the room. We normally keep it set between **2 and 3**. Setting 5 will run continuously and pumps out a *lot* of heat — use it if you really need to warm the place up fast, but it's rarely necessary.
